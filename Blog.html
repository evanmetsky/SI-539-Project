Breakfast in Pittsburgh

November 29, 2017 / by Marcy

Breakfast is my favorite meal to cook and prepare myself, and I most enjoy making omelettes and pancakes. My favorite breakfast dish is eggs benedict, but usually when I eat breakfast I have coffee, fruit, oatmeal, or a bagel.

I grew up in Pittsburgh, Pa, which is a city that is known for its sports teams and its restaurants. Pittsburgh is home to a few famous diners including DeLuca’s, which has been in Pittsburgh’s historic Strip District since 1950, and Pamela’s. Pamela’s Diner was briefly in the national spotlight in 2008 because, during a campaign stop in Pittsburgh, Barack and Michelle Obama visited a Pamela’s location for breakfast. In May 2009, Mrs. Obama invited Pamela’s co-owners, Gail Klingensmith and Pamela Cohen, to cook breakfast at the White House.

Although Pamela’s is one of the most famous diners in Pittsburgh, my favorite places to have breakfast are Zenith and Enrico’s. Zenith is an antique store, but on Sunday it is transformed into a brunch buffet! Brunch-goers are surrounded by vintage clothing and old-timey paintings as they navigate the buffet of vegan salads and cakes. Enrico’s is a small Italian bistro and bakery in Pittsburgh’s famous Strip District that serves breakfast only on Saturdays. I really like their fresh bread, coffee, and “greens and beans” dish.

Pittsburgh is a great city that has great food, and options for breakfast food are especially fantastic!
